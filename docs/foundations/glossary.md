---
title: "Glossary"
---

## Adaptation

The beneficial change produced when training stress is followed by sufficient
recovery.

## Athleticism

The coordinated expression of strength, speed, power, balance, and movement
options in changing conditions.

## General Exercise Physiology

Established exercise-science principles not attributed to one of the project's
approved expert sources.

## Movement Quality

The ability to reach and control positions appropriate to a task without
unnecessary compensation or pain.

## Paddle Capacity

The ability to sustain and repeat effective paddling while maintaining position,
technique, and recovery.

## Readiness

The athlete's current capacity to benefit from the planned training or surf
stress, considering recovery, symptoms, and accumulated fatigue.

## Relative Strength

Force-producing ability in relation to body mass.

## Repetitions in Reserve

An estimate of how many technically sound repetitions could still be completed
at the end of a set.

## Surf Athlete

A surfer who develops physical qualities to improve performance, durability,
recovery, and long-term capacity in the water. See [The Surf Athlete](surf-athlete.md).

## System

A reusable method or set of rules, distinct from a program that schedules its
application.

# Terminology

## Purpose

Keep language consistent across the manual.

## Preferred Terms

- **Surf Athlete:** capitalize when naming the project identity; use "surfer" in
  ordinary descriptions.
- **Paddle capacity:** the broad ability to sustain and repeat paddling. Use
  "paddle volume" only for the measured amount performed.
- **Movement quality:** controlled access to task-appropriate positions.
- **Mobility:** usable range of motion, not passive flexibility alone.
- **Relative strength:** strength considered in relation to body mass.
- **Readiness:** present capacity to absorb and adapt to stress.
- **Recovery:** the processes and conditions that enable adaptation.
- **System:** a reusable method.
- **Program:** a time-organized application of systems.
- **Performance opportunity:** a generic trip, competition, camp, or season for
  which a surfer may prepare.

## Programming Language

- Prefer "leave repetitions in reserve" over "push to the limit."
- Prefer "stop or modify" over language that encourages training through pain.
- Use relative timing such as "12 weeks before the performance opportunity" in
  public programs.
- Distinguish expected training discomfort from pain, altered mechanics, or
  symptoms that require modification.

## Public Language

Use generic examples and athlete roles. Do not include a person's age, body
weight, destination, calendar date, medical history, equipment inventory, or
benchmark unless it is part of a properly anonymized research source.

## Purpose

Define the physical identity this manual develops.

## Why It Matters for Surfing

Training decisions become clearer when every quality is judged by its value in
the water rather than by standards borrowed from another sport.

## Definition

A Surf Athlete is a surfer who deliberately develops the physical qualities
needed to paddle, position, recover, produce force, coordinate movement, and
remain durable across sessions and years.

## Capabilities

A Surf Athlete develops:

- Paddle capacity and efficient repeat efforts
- Aerobic fitness and recovery between efforts
- Relative strength without unnecessary mass or fatigue
- Daily practice of usable mobility and movement options
- Trunk, shoulder, hip, knee, and ankle durability
- Surf athleticism: power, balance, coordination, reaction, footwork, and rapid
  transitions
- Readiness awareness and recovery habits

## Training Priorities

Paddling is the primary sport-specific conditioning method. Strength,
conditioning, mobility, recovery, and nutrition support surfing rather than
competing with it. Training is successful when it improves capability while
preserving readiness for water time and future cycles.

Relative strength is preferred over hypertrophy as an end in itself. Body
composition remains an outcome of the complete system rather than the reason to
organize training.

## What the Surf Athlete Avoids

- Specializing in gym performance at the expense of surfing
- Chasing muscle size that does not improve performance or durability
- Treating exhaustion as proof of an effective session
- Adding conditioning that compromises paddle quality or recovery
- Forcing painful positions or loading movement that lacks control
- Peaking once by undermining long-term development

# Surf Athleticism

## Purpose

Develop the general physical qualities that help a surfer express movement
quickly, accurately, and adaptively in changing conditions.

## Why It Matters for Surfing

Surfing requires more than steady conditioning or gym strength. The surfer must
coordinate a rapid pop-up, organize stance and foot pressure, rotate, react to
the board and wave, and repeatedly regain useful positions without excessive
conscious effort.

## Scope

Surf athleticism includes:

- Pop-up speed and repeatability
- Balance and pressure control
- Footwork and stance transitions
- Rotation and separation
- Reaction and coordination
- Power expressed without unnecessary fatigue
- Grip and single-leg control where they support surf tasks

This system does not replace surfing practice. Dry-land work supports physical
options, while time in the water develops perception, timing, and surf skill.

## Programming Principles

- Practice athletic qualities while movement remains crisp.
- Use low enough volume to preserve surfing, paddling, and recovery.
- Stop a set when speed, coordination, or position degrades.
- Prefer task-relevant variability over fatigue-driven circuits.
- Progress complexity only after the underlying position is controlled.

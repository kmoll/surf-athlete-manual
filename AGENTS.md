## Project Mission

Build a body that lets you surf better every year.

Preserve both time horizons: readiness for the next meaningful surf opportunity
and lifelong improvement. The public repository must remain reusable and must
not contain an individual athlete's dates, destinations, measurements, medical
details, equipment constraints, benchmarks, or logs.

## Core Programming Rules

- Recovery has priority over planned volume.
- Movement quality and low-dose mobility are practiced daily, scaled to symptoms
  and recovery.
- Paddling is the primary sport-specific conditioning method.
- Norwegian 4x4 intervals are a VO2 max tool, not a mandatory session; their use
  is subordinate to surf volume, paddle volume, and recovery.
- Do not prescribe underwater breath-hold sets, maximum breath holds,
  hyperventilation, or unsupervised CO2-tolerance training. Follow the safety
  boundary in `docs/conditioning/index.md`.
- Strength supports surfing and should not create unnecessary soreness or fatigue.
- Heavy strength work remains low volume and technically sound.
- Avoid training to failure and leave repetitions in reserve.
- Preserve capacity for an additional hard session at the end of most weeks.
- Peak for important surf opportunities without compromising the next cycle.
- Body composition is an outcome of the system, not its organizing principle.

## Repository Rules

- Markdown files are canonical; GitHub is the authoritative version history.
- PDFs and websites are generated artifacts.
- Keep foundations, timeless systems, and program applications distinct.
- Treat `docs/foundations/project-north-star.md` as the sole canonical philosophy.
- Define protocols once and link to them with relative links.
- Keep personal details out of the public repository.
- Update `mkdocs.yml` when adding or moving navigable pages.
- Update `CHANGELOG.md` for meaningful changes and `TODO.md` for active work.
- Add a decision record for significant architecture or programming decisions.

## Content Rules

- Explain why each recommendation exists and how it transfers to surfing.
- Use the chapter and exercise templates in
  `docs/foundations/writing-standards.md` where applicable.
- Prefer concise, reusable documents.
- Preserve terminology defined in `docs/foundations/terminology.md`.
- Do not silently introduce new coaches or training systems.
- Do not convert the manual into bodybuilding.
- Do not add volume merely to make a program appear harder.
- State uncertainty and avoid unsupported claims.
